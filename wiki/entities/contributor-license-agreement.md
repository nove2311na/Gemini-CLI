# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

A Contributor License Agreement (CLA) is a mandatory legal requirement for contributing code to Gemini CLI. This agreement between the contributor (or their employer) and Google permits the project to use and redistribute contributed code.

## Purpose

The CLA does not transfer copyright ownership from the contributor. Instead, it grants Google permission to use and redistribute the contribution as part of the project. This legal foundation enables Google to incorporate contributions into the project without facing licensing complications.

## Requirement

No pull request can be merged without a signed CLA on file. This applies to all contributors, including project members. The requirement exists regardless of whether the contribution comes from an individual or an organization.

## Existing Agreements

If you (or your employer) have previously signed a Google CLA for another project, you likely do not need to sign again. The Google CLA system tracks agreements globally, so prior signatures apply to future contributions across different projects.

## How to Sign

Visit [https://cla.developers.google.com/](https://cla.developers.google.com/) to see your current agreements or to initiate a new signature. The process involves reviewing the agreement terms and providing electronic acknowledgment of acceptance.
