## 4. MARKET INSIGHT

### The SME Automation Gap

Large enterprises hire automation engineers or pay IBM/Oracle consultants. Startups build custom code. The gap in between - businesses with 30-200 employees who need real automation but can't afford enterprise consulting and don't want to learn n8n themselves - is almost entirely unserved.

The dominant "competitors" in this space are:
1. Freelance developers building one-off scripts (unreliable, no ongoing support)
2. Enterprise software vendors pushing feature-heavy platforms (too complex for the team)
3. AI chatbot agencies selling chatbots (narrow scope, no operational integration)

**Our opportunity:** Position as the trusted operational partner who maps, automates, and maintains the business processes SME owners don't want to think about.

## 8. SERVICE PACKAGING

### Tier 1 - Entry Automation (VND 80-150M)
**Best for:** Single workflow that causes recurring pain
**Typical scope:** One core process automated end-to-end

**Examples:**
- Lead capture from website form to auto-populate Airtable to trigger Zalo notification to sales lead to log follow-up task
- Weekly inventory count from WhatsApp to auto-update Airtable to alert ops manager if stock < threshold
- Quote request to auto-email with pricing sheet to track open/click to reminder if no reply in 24h

**Delivery:** 4-6 weeks
**Pricing basis:** 2-3 weeks of work x day rate (VND 3-5M/day effective)
**What client gets:** Process map, working automation, 30-day support, handover documentation

### Tier 2 - Growth Automation (VND 250-450M)
**Best for:** 2-3 connected workflows across lead management and operations
**Typical scope:** Full lead-to-cash or ops management automation

**What's included:**
- Process audit (we map 3-5 workflows, identify quick wins)
- 2-3 automations built and integrated
- Dashboard showing key metrics (lead response time, quote-to-close rate)
- 3-month retainer for maintenance and small adjustments
- Training session for staff (1 hour)

**Delivery:** 8-12 weeks
**Retainer after:** VND 25-40M/month

### Tier 3 - Operational Partner (VND 150-250M/month)
**Best for:** SME owners who want an ongoing automation function without hiring staff

**What's included:**
- Monthly process review (identify new automatable workflows)
- Priority access (we handle your requests within 3 business days)

- Quarterly report: time saved, errors avoided, pipeline impact

## 10. MESSAGING PILLARS

### Pillar 1 - Time Liberation
**Core message:** "We automate the work that keeps you from doing the work that matters."

**Sales example phrases to use in discovery calls:**
- *"A furniture factory in Binh Duong was spending 12 hours every week manually copying WhatsApp messages into a Google Sheet. We automated that in 3 weeks. Now their sales team just responds to leads instead of typing them."*
- *"Your competitor responds to new leads in 15 minutes. You take 3 hours. That's not a people problem — it's a process problem. We fix the process."*
- *"Every time someone on your team manually sends a status update, that's money down the drain. We've helped clients recover 8-12 hours per week per automated workflow."*

**Usage:** Lead with time reclaim in first conversation. Let the prospect calculate their own cost: "How many hours does your team spend on [X] each week? What is each hour worth to you?"

---

### Pillar 2 - Audit Before Build
**Core message:** "We don't start building until we understand how you actually work."

**Sales example phrases to use in discovery calls:**
- *"Before we touch a single tool, we spend a week watching how your team actually works — not how it should work on paper. Then we show you exactly where the time leaks are."*
- *"Most automation vendors ask you what you want built. We ask what breaks. There's a difference — one gives you a tool, the other gives you a solution."*
- *"We start every engagement with a process map your team can actually use. Even if you don't hire us, you'll know where your bottlenecks are."*

**Usage:** Use this pillar during the audit call itself. Show the prospect you care about their reality before proposing anything. Builds trust before asking for the sale.

---

### Pillar 3 - Human-in-the-Loop Design
**Core message:** "Your team is involved. Your data stays yours. Your approval is required."

**Sales example phrases to use in discovery calls:**
- *"The automation doesn't decide anything on its own. It moves the work to the right person and tells them what needs their attention. Your team makes the final call."*
- *"We don't disappear after delivery. You'll always be able to see what happened, when, and why — in a dashboard, not a code log."*
- *"If your team stops using it, we fix it. That's why we design automations around how your people actually work, not how we'd prefer them to work."*

**Usage:** Deploy when the prospect shows signs of automation fear ("But what if it breaks?" / "I need to keep control"). This pillar directly addresses that objection.

---

### Pillar 4 - Outcome Guarantee
**Core message:** "If the automation doesn't work as described within 30 days, we fix it free."

**Sales example phrases to use in proposals:**
- *"We fix it free for 30 days after launch — not because we're generous, but because we know it works. We audit before we build, so we know the automation will do what we promised."*
- *"The guarantee exists because we don't build anything until we've mapped your process. That mapping is our quality control — it tells us exactly what we're building and why it will work."*
- *"If the automation stops working, you don't wait in a queue. You message us directly. That's what the retainer covers — not just the tools, but the maintenance."*

**Usage:** Put this in every proposal and on the 1-pager. Differentiates sharply from freelance developers who deliver and disappear, and from agencies who shrug and say "that's out of scope."

## 13. CAMPAIGN IDEAS

### Campaign 1 - "The Automation Audit" (Lead Magnet)
- **Offer:** Free 30-min process audit with written recommendations (VND 3-5M value, delivered free)
- **Target:** SME owners who feel overwhelmed by manual ops but don't know where to start
- **Funnel:** Facebook/LinkedIn ad to landing page with Calendly to audit call to proposal
- **Why it works:** Low-commitment entry; positions us as process experts before selling anything

### Campaign 2 - "Process Owner" LinkedIn Content Series
- **Format:** 6-post series, one industry pain point per post
- **Content:** "What happens in a typical [manufacturing/logistics] company on Monday morning" to "Here's where the time actually goes" to "This is where automation pays off"
- **Goal:** Thought leadership + lead generation simultaneously
- **Frequency:** 1 post per week for 6 weeks

### Campaign 3 - Referral Partnership
- **Target partners:** Webflow/Supabase developers, business consultants, HR firms, accounting firms
- **Mechanism:** 10% referral fee on first project revenue; co-marketing for mutual clients
- **Why partners win:** They serve the same clients and have credibility we lack
- **Pitch:** "We handle the automation so you can focus on your core service"

### Campaign 4 - Industry-Specific Case Study
- **Format:** 500-800 word case study with metrics, process before/after, client quote
- **Target industry:** Manufacturing SME (first client or test client)
- **Distribution:** LinkedIn post + email to warm network + industry Facebook groups
- **Why it works:** The most persuasive thing we can show is a similar business that got results

### Campaign 5 - Cold Outreach Sequence (Email + LinkedIn)
- **Target:** Operations managers at manufacturing and logistics SMEs (VND 20-100B revenue)
- **Sequence:** Value-add (process insight, not pitch) to social proof (case study) to offer (audit call)
- **Tools:** Apollo.io or LinkedIn Sales Navigator for list building
- **Target:** 200 outreach touches in 30 days; goal 5 responses, 2 calls

## 16. FIRST CLIENT ACQUISITION

### Tactic 1 - Process Audit at Cost
Offer to do a free process audit (VND 3-5M value) for 2-3 warm network prospects. Deliver a written recommendation document. Use the audit as a conversion mechanism - if they like the insights, offer to implement the #1 recommendation at a discount (Tier 1 price).

### Tactic 2 - "First Project" Pricing
Offer a discounted entry project (VND 60-80M instead of VND 80-150M) for the first 3 clients, contingent on: (a) they provide a case study quote and (b) they make one warm referral. This gives us social proof and reduces the first-mover risk.

### Tactic 3 - Test Client Swap
Approach one warm contact who runs a suitable SME. Offer a significant discount in exchange for: being our documented case study, allowing us to film a short Loom walkthrough, and being available for one referral call with their network.

### Tactic 4 - Industry Event Attendance
Attend one SME-focused event per month in HCMC or target city. Aim for 5 meaningful conversations per event. Bring printed 1-pager and process audit template as leave-behinds.

### Tactic 5 - LinkedIn DM to Warm Warmth
Use 2nd-degree connections on LinkedIn to find warm introductions to target ICP. The message: "I noticed you know [X]. I'm helping SMEs automate their operations - would [X] mind making an intro?"

```
Trigger: Khách hàng gửi tin nhắn Zalo "Báo giá [sản phẩm]"
↓
Action: Auto-log vào Airtable (khách, sản phẩm, ngày, nguồn)

↓
Action: Alert nhân viên sales qua Zalo notification
↓
Action: Sau 2h: Auto gửi báo giá (template) + theo dõi mở email
↓
Action: Nếu không phản hồi trong 24h → Reminder cho sales
```

**Workflow 2: Inventory Alert**
```
Trigger: Nhân viên gửi tin nhắn Zalo "Tồn kho [mã] = [số lượng]"
↓
Action: Auto-update Airtable inventory tracker
↓
Action: Kiểm tra vs threshold (từ Airtable)
↓
If tồn kho < threshold:
  → Alert ops manager qua Zalo: "Sắp hết hàng [mã] - còn [số lượng]"
  → Tạo task mua hàng trong Notion/Airtable
```

**ROI Example:**
- Sales lead response: từ 2-4 giờ → 15 phút
- Time saved: 8-10 giờ/tuần (VND 2-3M worth)
- Entry price: VND 80-120M
- Payback: 4-6 tháng
